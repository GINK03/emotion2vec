Neuromorphic Computing Platform (NCP) consists of two complementary and configurable neuromorphic computing systems, based on custom hardware designs.\nThese systems are designed to emulate neural microcircuits and __apply brain-like principles in machine learning and cognitive computing, i.e. principles that will allow the machine to learn in __the way that the brain does rather than be programmed like a normal computer_.\nTo do this, the Neuromorphic Computing Platform uses state-of-the-art electronic component and circuit technologies and incorporates new knowledge gained from other areas of neuroscience research, e.g. experimental neuroscience, theoretical neuroscience and brain modelling.\nThe Neuromorphic Computing Platform provides remote access to large-scale neuromorphic computing systems based in Manchester, UK and Heidelberg, Germany. \nTogether with the TrueNorth system by IBM these constitute the only neuromorphic systems in the world capable of running simulations of neural circuit systems with state-of-the-art models of neurons,synapses and plasticity in either real time or accelerated time (i.e. 10,000 times faster than real time).\nThe Manchester system is a Multi-Core Neuromorphic System, built on the work of the UK SpiNNaker project.\nThis system is based on individual chips, each with 18 ARM cores, consisting of 500,000 processors. Platform](https://www.humanbrainproject.eu/ncp)\n\n>__The Neuromorphic Computing Platform (NCP)__ consists of __two complementary and configurable neuromorphic computing systems__, based on custom hardware designs.\n>\n>These systems are designed __to emulate neural microcircuit__s and ___apply brain-like principles in machine learning and cognitive computing__, i.e. principles that will allow the machine to learn in __the way that the brain does rather than be programmed like a normal computer__.\n>\n>To do this, the Neuromorphic Computing Platform __uses state-of-the-art electronic component and circuit technologies__ and incorporates new knowledge gained from other areas of neuroscience research, e.g. __experimental neuroscience, theoretical neuroscience and brain modelling__.\n>\n>The Neuromorphic Computing Platform provides remote access to large-scale neuromorphic computing systems based in __Manchester, UK and Heidelberg, Germany__. \n>\n>__Together with the TrueNorth system by IBM__ these constitute the only neuromorphic systems in the world capable of running simulations of neural circuit systems with state-of-the-art models of neurons,synapses and plasticity in either real time or accelerated time (i.e. 10,000 times faster than real time).\n>\n>__The Manchester system__ is __a Multi-Core Neuromorphic System__, built on the work of __the UK SpiNNaker project__.\n>\n> This system is based on individual chips, each with 18 ARM cores, consisting of 500,000 processors.
