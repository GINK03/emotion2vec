Mission\u30a6\u30a7\u30d6\u30da\u30fc\u30b8\n\n\nMission\nEvery one of us knows someone who is struggling with a brain related illness: Parkinson\u2019s, Alzheimer\u2019s, Autism, dementia, depression, brain tumors and many more.\nDoctors around the world have unmet needs, for devices and medicines that could potentially turn the lives of their patients around, and even save them.\nIn parallel, there are innovators and researchers that have brilliant ideas for developing breakthrough technologies in the brain arena.  And there are entrepreneurs, looking for a breakthrough product in BrainTech.\nSometimes the most important thing we can do is bring all these people together, so that they can communicate and synchronize the medical needs, the ideas and the business platforms together.\nIn 2010 Israel\u2019s President Shimon Peres envisioned creating a brain technology hub in Israel.  Israel Brain Technologies (IBT) is his brainchild \u2013 a non-profit organization which mission is to bring doctors, innovators, researchers, entrepreneurs and investors in the BrainTech arena together, so that patients will get access to new treatments, faster.\nIBT works out of Israel, which is ideally positioned to be a leading BrainTech hub.  Israel\u2019s skilled workforce and entrepreneurial spirit has already made it a global leader in hi-tech, water technology and medical devices.  IBT brings together the leading minds in Israel, and from around the world, for the benefit of patients.\n\n\n\n\u7528\u8a9e\u6574\u7406\n\nBrain Technology\n\nWikipedia Brain technology\n\n\nBrain technology, or self-learning know-how systems, defines a technology that employs latest findings in neuroscience. \nThe term was first introduced by the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory in Zurich, Switzerland, in the context of the Roboy project.[1] Brain Technology can be employed in robots,[2] know-how management systems[3] and any other application with self-learning capabilities. \nIn particular, Brain Technology applications allow the visualization of the underlying learning architecture often coined as \u201cknow-how maps\u201d.\n\nArtificial brain\n\nWikipedia Artificial brain\n\n\nArtificial brain (or artificial mind) is a term commonly used in the media[1] to describe research that aims to develop software and hardware with cognitive abilities similar to those of the animal or human brain. \nResearch investigating \"artificial brains\" and brain emulation plays three important roles in science:\n\nAn ongoing attempt by neuroscientists to understand how the human brain works, known as cognitive neuroscience.\nA thought experiment in the philosophy of artificial intelligence, demonstrating that it is possible, at least in theory, to create a machine that has all the capabilities of a human being.\nA long term project to create machines exhibiting behavior comparable to those of animals with complex central nervous system such as mammals and most particularly humans. An ongoing attempt by neuroscientists to understand how the human brain works, known as cognitive neuroscience.\n> 2.
