algorithms\nNeural networks are closely connected to the inductive modelling, although they have different background. \nThe GMDH evolved from the mathematical description of a system by means of Kolmogorov-Gabor polynomial [12].\nNeural networks were at the beginning biologically oriented. \nLater, powerful optimization methods for neural networks (Back-Propagation of error) were invented, allowing building multilayered networks of neurons (MLP) capable of solving nonlinear problems.\nIt has been shown [13] that MLPs are equivalent to mathematical description of a system by means of the Koglomorov theorem (although inner functions are very complex and they have almost fractal character).\nIn recent time both neural networks and GMDH algorithms are optimized by genetic algorithms and it is even harder to distinguish the boundary between both theories.\nOf course, some neural networks are very different from GMDH (recurrent, modular, spiking neural networks, etc.) [14].\nThe article [15] shows that the problem of two intertwined spirals can be successfully solved by the MultiLayered Perceptron (MLP), where weights are evolved by the Genetic Algorithm.\nNumber of function evaluation is in this case much higher than when using standard BackPropagation, because the information about gradient of error is not utilized in the GA. On the other hand, in some\napplications, Genetic approach gives better results than BackPropagation [16].\nThe Cascade Correlation algorithm [17] is capable of solving extremely difficult problems.\nIt performs optimally on \"spiral\" benchmarking problem (a network consisting of less than 20 neurons is generated). \nAccording to experiments on real-world data performed in [18], the algorithm has difficulties with avoiding premature convergence to complex topological structures.\nThe main advantage of the Cascade Correlation algorithm is also its main disadvantage.\nIt easily solves extremely difficult problems therefore it is likely to overfit. \nThe recent article [19] proposes an improvement of the Cascade Correlation Algorithm [17]. \nThe original algorithm assumes fully connected network.\nEach neuron is connected to all features and all previously built neurons.\nThe improvement called Evolving Cascade Correlation Networks (ECCN)[19] uses techniques from GMDH theory [1] to choose just relevant inputs for each neuron. \nCascade networks evolved by ECCN overfit data less than fully connected cascade networks.\nRecently, very interesting algorithm for designing recurrent neural networks was proposed in [20, 21].\nThe NeuroEvolution Through Augmenting Topologies (NEAT) is designed for solving reinforcement learning tasks [22], but can be applied also to supervised learning problems.\nSimilarly to the GMDH, NEAT networks grow from a minimal structure up to optimal complexity.\nThe topology and also weights of the NEAT networks are evolved using niching genetic algorithm[23].\nThe NEAT is primary designed for reinforcement learning.\nIt can also solve standard supervised problems.\nWe have applied the NEAT to Two intertwined spiral problem [24], but it failed.\nThe reason why NEAT is unable to evolve successful networks solving the spiral problem is probably that a) the chromosome is too big when evolving architecture and weights of network simultaneously, b) niching alone is unable to protect complex structures. \n\n\u4e0a\u8a18\u306e\u8ad6\u6587\u306e\u307b\u304b\u306b\u3001NeuroEvoltuion \u3068 GMDH \u306e Algorithms\n\n\nAbstract\nGroup Method of Data Handling (GMDH)-type neural network algorithms are the heuristic self organization method for the modelling of complex systems. \nGMDH algorithms are utilized for a variety of purposes, examples include identification of physical laws, the extrapolation of physical fields, pattern recognition, clustering, the approximation of multidimensional processes, forecasting without models, etc.\nIn this study, the R package GMDH is presented to make short term forecasting through GMDH-type neural network algorithms. \nThe GMDH package has options to use different transfer functions (sigmoid, radial basis, polynomial, and tangent functions) simultaneously or separately. \nData on cancer death rate of Pennsylvania from 1930 to 2000 are used to illustrate the features of the GMDH package. The results based on ARIMA models and exponential smoothing methods are included for comparison.\n\n\n\uff08 ELESVIER \uff09Isa Ebtehaja et.al, GMDH-type neural network approach for modeling the Nariman-zadehb, Evolutionary design of generalized GMDH-type neural network for prediction of concrete compressive strength using UPV\n\n\nAbstract\nThe main purpose of this paper is to predict the insitu compressive strength of concrete by means of non-destructive approach using ultrasonic pulse velocity (UPV) method. \nFor this purpose generalized GMDH-type (group method of data handling) neural network was developed based on various data obtained experimentally. \nEvolutionary algorithms (EAs) are deployed for optimal design of GMDH-type neural networks.\nA set of experimental data for the training and testing the evolved GMDH-type neural network is employed in which ultrasonic pulse velocity (UPV), concrete age, water\u2013cement ratio and fine/coarse aggregate ratio are considered as inputs and concrete compressive strength is regarded as the output variables.\nSensitivity analysis has also been carried out on one of the obtaining models to study the influence of input parameters on model output. \nThe results show that generalized GMDH-type neural network has a great ability as a feasible tool for prediction of the concrete compressive strength.\n\n##__\u203b [18], the algorithm has difficulties with avoiding premature convergence to complex topological structures.\n>\n> The main advantage of the Cascade Correlation algorithm is also its main disadvantage.\n>\n> It easily solves extremely difficult problems therefore it is likely to overfit. \n>\n> The recent article [19] proposes an improvement of the Cascade Correlation Algorithm [17]. \n>\n> The original algorithm assumes fully connected network.\n> \n> Each neuron is connected to all features and all previously built neurons.\n>\n>  The improvement called Evolving Cascade cascade networks.\n>\n> **Recently, very interesting algorithm for designing recurrent neural networks was proposed in [20, 21].**\n>\n> **The NeuroEvolution Through Augmenting Topologies (NEAT)** is
