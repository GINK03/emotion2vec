processing (and action selection) can be explained by maximizing Bayesian model evidence or minimizing variational free energy\u2014we ask whether neuronal responses can be described as a gradient well-characterized neuronal phenomena.\nThese include repetition suppression, mismatch negativity, violation responses, place-cell activity, phase precession, theta sequences, theta-gamma cou- pling, of interoceptive inference means that bodily states are regulated by autonomic reflexes that are enslaved by descending predictions from deep generative models of our internal and external milieu.\nThis re-conceptualization illuminates several issues in cognitive and clinical neuroscience with implications for experiences of selfhood and emotion.\nWe first contextualize interoception in neuromodulation.\nWe next examine the implications for understanding the functional anatomy of the emotional brain, surveying recent observations on agranular cortex.\nFinally, we turn to theoretical
