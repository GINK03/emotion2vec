Tajima, Ryota Kanai\n(Submitted on 18 Jan 2017) Integrated information and dimensionality in continuous attractor dynamics\n\n\nThere has been increasing interest in the integrated information theory (IIT) of consciousness, which hypothesizes that consciousness emerges from integrated information processing by neuronal dynamics. \nHowever, the current formulation of IIT poses both practical and theoretical problems when we aim to empirically test the theory by computing integrated information from neuronal signals.\nFor example, measuring integrated information requires observing all the elements at the same time, but this is practically rather difficult.\n\uff08 \u4e2d\u7565 \uff09\nHere we propose that some aspects of such problems are resolved by considering the topological dimensionality of shared attractor dynamics as an indicator of integrated information in continuous attractor dynamics. \nIn this formulation, the effects of unobserved nodes on the attractor dynamics can be reconstructed using a technique called delay-embedding, which allows us to determine the dimensionality of an embedded attractor from partial observations.\nWe propose that the topological dimensionality indicates a critical property of integrated information as it is invariant to general coordinate transformations.
